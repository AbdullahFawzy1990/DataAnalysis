Project: Investigate a Dataset (No-show appointments)¶

Exploring how multiple factors are associated with patients not attending appointments

Glossary¶

  • Poor patients/ Low income patients: Patients on Bolsa Família Scholarship scheme. Scholarship is a part of a social welfare program

  • Wait-time: Number of days between scheduling an appointment and attending it

  • Absence rate: percent of patients who not show up to medical appointment

Table of Contents¶

  • Introduction
  • Data Wrangling
  • Exploratory Data Analysis
  • Conclusions

Introduction¶

The "No-show appointments" dataset provides information on 100K+ patients in Brazil. The main question to be raised is which factors are associated with patients scheduling doctors appointments while not showing up to them. Around 10 different variables are provided to help answer this question.

In the next sections, I'll start by posing the main questions I brainstormed by just looking at which variables are available to explore. Questions may make use of one or more variables simultaneously. Next, will move into data wrangling to clean the data and make it ready to use. I'll follow this with exploring the research questions through some analysis and visualizations.
I will end the report with the main conclusions I reached

Research Questions¶

  1. How does patients' age correlate with showing up to appointment? Does patients' gender affect this relationship?
  2. What is the relation between wait time and patients absence rate?
  3. Are poor patients more inclined to miss appointments than other patients?
  4. How do medical conditions (Hipertension, Diabetes, and/or Alcoholism) affect patients' commitment to attend appointments?

def date_difference (dataframe, column_1, column_2):
    """
    Calculate the number of days between two dates (column_1 - column_2). 
    
    Inputs:
    dataframe: Pandas dataframe that contain date columns
    column_1: first date column
    column_2: second date column
    
    Outputs: 
    number_of_days: a series of values that represents date differences
    """
    
    number_of_days = pd.Series(range(len(df)))
    counter=0    
   
    for c, d in zip (dataframe[column_1], dataframe[column_2]):

        year_1, year_2 = int(c[0:4]), int(d[0:4])
        month_1, month_2 = int(c[5:7]), int(d[5:7])
        day_1, day_2 = int(c[8:10]), int(d[8:10])
        
        delta = date(year_1, month_1,day_1) - date(year_2, month_2,day_2)
        result = delta.days

        number_of_days.loc[counter] = result
        counter += 1
        
    return number_of_days

Conclusions¶

The main idea behind this dataset is to explore factors that may be associated with patients not showing up to doctors' appointments. It goes without saying that correlation doesn't entail causation. However, some factors seem to be linked with not showing up to appointments.

Findings:¶

  1. Patients from different age groups seem have different levels of commitment regarding medical appointments. This is true for both males and females.
  2. There is a positive correlation between length of wait-time and absence rate. More waiting time(in days) before appointments generally leads to more patients not showing up.
  3. Low income families has more tendency to miss medical appointments than others. They're also generally less responsive to SMS reminders
  4. No obvious relation between patients' health issues and absence rate.

Limitations¶

  • Since 10 factors are available, it's very tough to make clear cut conclusions on the data. This is because there are dozens of different relationships to explore beyond this assignemnt. For this reason, other tools like machine learning & neural networks can be helpful.
  • Other Socio-economic factors, i.e. income, local health system, etc., may be helpful to understand relationships more deeply.
